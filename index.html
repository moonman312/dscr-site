What the debt service coverage ratio tells you

What DSCR measures

The debt service coverage ratio answers the question every lender asks: does this property earn enough to cover its loan? It's net operating income divided by annual debt service. At 1.0x, NOI exactly covers the payment with nothing to spare; at 1.25x there's a 25% cushion; below 1.0x the property runs a shortfall on its debt.

The formula, with an example

DSCR = NOI ÷ annual debt service. If a property earns $1,000,000 of NOI and its loan costs $800,000 a year in principal and interest, DSCR = 1,000,000 ÷ 800,000 = 1.25x. To work backward to a target, flip it: required NOI = annual debt service × target DSCR — so hitting 1.35x on that loan would take $1,080,000 of NOI.

What counts as a good DSCR

There's no universal number, but the rules of thumb are consistent: below 1.00x doesn't cover the debt; 1.00–1.20x is thin; 1.20–1.35x is often acceptable depending on lender and property; 1.35x and above is healthier. Hotels, which have more volatile cash flow than most property types, often face higher requirements.

How to improve your DSCR

Three levers move it: raise NOI (more revenue or lower costs), reduce the loan (a paydown lowers the payment), or change the terms (a lower rate, longer amortization, or an interest-only period). Which is realistic depends on your situation — the hotel diagnostic shows how far each one moves your ratio.

More terms worth knowing

Capital stack

The layers of money in a deal, ranked by who gets paid first: senior debt, then mezzanine, then preferred equity, then your common (owner) equity.

Defeasance

A way to retire some fixed-rate (often CMBS) loans early by substituting a portfolio of securities that replicates the remaining payments. Can be costly when rates have fallen.

Yield maintenance

A prepayment penalty that compensates the lender for the interest it loses if you pay off early.

PIP (Property Improvement Plan)

A brand-required renovation scope and budget, often triggered at acquisition or franchise renewal.

Questions, answered

What is DSCR (debt service coverage ratio)?
DSCR, the debt service coverage ratio, measures whether a property's income covers its debt. It equals net operating income (NOI) divided by annual debt service. A DSCR of 1.25x means NOI is 1.25 times the annual loan payment; below 1.00x means the property doesn't earn enough to cover the loan.
What is the DSCR formula?
DSCR = Net Operating Income (NOI) ÷ Annual Debt Service. NOI is operating income before debt and capital costs; annual debt service is a full year of principal and interest payments. Worked example: $1,000,000 NOI ÷ $800,000 annual debt service = 1.25x DSCR.
How do you calculate DSCR?
Divide annual NOI by annual debt service (all 12 monthly payments). Example: NOI of $1,000,000 and debt service of $800,000 gives 1,000,000 ÷ 800,000 = 1.25x. Use the calculator above to do it instantly.
What is a good DSCR?
Lenders generally want 1.25x or higher. Below 1.00x the property doesn't cover its debt from operations; 1.00–1.20x is thin coverage; 1.20–1.35x may be acceptable depending on the lender and asset; 1.35x and above is considered healthier.
What's the difference between DSCR and debt yield?
DSCR (NOI ÷ debt service) depends on your interest rate and amortization, so it changes with loan terms. Debt yield (NOI ÷ loan amount) ignores the terms, giving lenders a rate-proof measure of leverage — commonly with a 9–10% floor.

The formula

DSCR = Net Operating Income ÷ Annual Debt Service. A 1.25x DSCR means the property's NOI is 1.25 times its annual loan payment.

What's a good DSCR?

Below 1.00x doesn't cover the loan; 1.00–1.20x is thin; 1.20–1.35x may be acceptable; 1.35x and up is generally healthier.

Why lenders use it

It tells a lender whether the property earns enough to cover its debt service before they agree to fund or refinance.

DSCR vs. debt yield

DSCR depends on your rate and amortization; debt yield (NOI ÷ loan) ignores them, so lenders use it as a rate-proof floor.

How to improve your DSCR

1

Raise NOI

Increase revenue (rate, occupancy, channel mix) or cut operating costs — every dollar of NOI lifts the ratio.

2

Reduce the loan

A principal paydown lowers annual debt service, which raises DSCR directly.

3

Change the terms

A lower rate, longer amortization, or an interest-only period each reduce the payment and improve coverage.
